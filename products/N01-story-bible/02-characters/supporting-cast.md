## Council & Authority Figures

### **Adric Voss**

**Role:** Council administrator; bureaucratic obstacle.

**Function:**
- Represents institutional response to chaos
- Creates friction for Jhace and Tiffani
- Demonstrates that Council doesn't understand magic
- Provides exposition about policy and restrictions

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Fifth smelting (late forties)
- Neat, formal dress; carries documentation everywhere
- Speaks in policy language; avoids emotional appeals
- Frustrated by magic's unpredictability

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 4: Interrogates Jhace about scaffold incident
- Ch 10: Attempts to restrict magic use via bureaucracy
- Ch 14: Present at climax; realizes policies are inadequate

**Memorable Trait:** Always says "Per Council regulation..." before speaking.

---

### **Enner Coil**

**Role:** Senior structural inspector; pragmatic voice.

**Function:**
- Provides technical expertise
- Supports Jhace professionally
- Represents older generation's skepticism about magic
- Grounds story in craft reality

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Sixth smelting (early fifties)
- Weathered, scarred hands; forge dust in hair
- Speaks bluntly; respects skill over theory
- Suspicious of magic but can't deny results

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 2: Inspects Jhace's repair work; grudging approval
- Ch 7: Warns Jhace about overuse; practical concern
- Ch 13: Collaborates with Tiffani on critical repair

**Memorable Trait:** Taps structural joints while thinking.

## Craft Workers & Community

### **Lessin Hode**

**Role:** Glassworks apprentice; Tiffani's assistant.

**Function:**
- Provides Tiffani with someone to teach and mentor
- Demonstrates cost through observation (sees Tiffani's stiffness)
- Asks questions that force Tiffani to articulate feelings
- Represents next generation learning craft

**Appearance/Manner:**
- First smelting (teens)
- Eager, observant, quick learner
- Asks too many questions; genuinely curious
- Loyal to Tiffani despite her demanding standards

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 4: Learns precision technique from Tiffani
- Ch 9: Notices Tiffani's hands shaking; offers help
- Ch 12: Completes work when Tiffani physically can't

**Memorable Trait:** Hums while working glass.

---

### **Keev and Ranna Torsen**

**Role:** Married scaffold workers; community anchors.

**Function:**
- Represent stable, healthy relationship (contrast to Jhace and Tiffani's tension)
- Provide community context and gossip
- Offer support without judgment
- Demonstrate impact of magic on ordinary people

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Both third smelting (late twenties to early thirties)
- Keev: Stocky, practical, good-humored
- Ranna: Tall, observant, mediator

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 1: Introduce Jhace's daily life and community
- Ch 6: Discuss magic use rumors; worried but hopeful
- Ch 11: Offer shelter and food; practical kindness

**Memorable Trait:** Finish each other's sentences; clearly devoted.

---

### **Prett Mallin**

**Role:** Former healer (pre-magic); skeptic.

**Function:**
- Represents medical establishment threatened by magic
- Creates ideological conflict (traditional vs. magical healing)
- Forces Jhace to defend his abilities
- Demonstrates fear of obsolescence

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Fourth smelting (late thirties)
- Precise, methodical; carries medical kit
- Defensive about traditional healing methods
- Secretly envious of Jhace's abilities

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 5: Confronts Jhace; accuses him of recklessness
- Ch 8: Forced to admit magical healing worked better
- Ch 14: Assists Jhace despite ideological differences

**Memorable Trait:** Always smells of medicinal herbs.

## Victims & Recipients of Magic

### **The Child on the Scaffold (Nael)**

**Role:** Early healing recipient; catalyst for vein-burn.

**Function:**
- Demonstrates Jhace's instinctive compassion
- Triggers first major cost (vein-burn appearance)
- Creates emotional stakes (Jhace couldn't live with himself if he let child die)
- Shows magic can succeed but always costs

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Very young (5-7 years old)
- Injured in scaffold collapse
- Terrified, crying; clutches Jhace afterward
- Parents forever grateful

**Key Scene:**
- Ch 3: Scaffold incident; Jhace heals Nael's injuries

**Memorable Trait:** Calls Jhace "the warm man" afterward.

---

### **Bram Torrins** (Deceased, appears in flashback/memory)

**Role:** Jhace's mentor; ghost haunting the narrative.

**Function:**
- Establishes Jhace's trauma and fear of failure
- Demonstrates limits of healing (couldn't save him)
- Provides backstory for vein-burn origins
- Creates emotional weight for Jhace's arc

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Would be sixth smelting if alive (fifties)
- Jhace's surrogate father figure
- Skilled structural worker; patient teacher
- Died of internal injuries Jhace couldn't heal

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 1: Jhace remembers Bram's lessons
- Ch 8: Flashback to failed healing attempt; Bram's death
- Ch 15: Jhace honors Bram's memory by accepting limits

**Memorable Trait:** Always said, "Measure twice, cut once."

## Philosophical & Social Voices

**Role:** Informal philosopher; Venn's friend.

**Function:**
- Provides philosophical counterpoint
- Debates ethics of magic use
- Represents "magic is dangerous" faction
- Creates tension without villainy

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Third smelting (late twenties)

- Genuinely worried about consequences

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 4: Debates Venn about magic ethics
- Ch 10: Argues for restrictions; fears chaos
- Ch 14: Forced to rely on magic; ideals vs. survival

**Memorable Trait:** Points dramatically while arguing.

## Antagonistic Forces (Not Villains)

### **Soress Tike**

**Role:** Reckless magic user; cautionary tale.

**Function:**
- Demonstrates consequences of overuse
- Creates fear and social tension
- Forces Council to respond with restrictions
- Shows Jhace and Tiffani what NOT to become

**Appearance/Manner:**
- Second to third smelting (mid-twenties)
- Visibly deteriorating (burns, tremors, exhaustion)
- Refuses to stop using magic despite costs
- Defensive, angry, desperate

**Key Scenes:**
- Ch 6: Uses magic recklessly; injures self and others
- Ch 10: Collapses in public; visible costs terrify community
- Ch 13: Near-death; Jhace attempts to help

**Memorable Trait:** Burns covering both arms; refuses to hide them.

## Summary

**Function by Category:**

**Authority (Adric, Enner):** Create friction, represent institutions, ground story in social context.

**Community (Keev, Ranna, Callai):** Provide emotional support, demonstrate social impact, represent stability.

**Craft Workers (Lessin, Prett):** Ground story in work reality, show generational dynamics, represent traditional vs. new.

**Victims/Recipients (Nael, Bram):** Create emotional stakes, demonstrate magic's impact, provide motivation.

**Philosophical (Derris, Venn):** Debate ethics, represent different viewpoints, force characters to articulate beliefs.

**Cautionary (Soress):** Demonstrate consequences, create urgency, show what happens without caution.

---

**Voice Differentiation:**

- **Bureaucrats** (Adric): Formal, policy-focused, emotionally distant
- **Craft workers** (Enner, Keev, Ranna): Practical, blunt, physically grounded
- **Philosophers** (Derris, Venn): Abstract, questioning, metaphorical
- **Optimists** (Callai, Lessin): Energetic, forward-looking, collaborative
- **Pessimists** (Prett, Derris): Defensive, cautious, traditional
- **Reckless** (Soress): Desperate, angry, denial-driven

---

**Naming Patterns:**

- Short, blunt names for craft workers (Jhace, Keev, Prett)
- Softer names for nurturers (Ranna, Callai, Lessin)
- Harsher names for conflict sources (Adric, Soress, Derris)
- All names fit Te'Oga linguistic patterns (hard consonants, simple vowels)
